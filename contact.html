Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers to the questions tech students ask us most often.

Do I need a Master's degree to work in AI?
No — while a Master's or PhD is advantageous for research roles, the majority of AI engineering and data science positions are accessible with a Bachelor's degree combined with relevant certifications and a strong portfolio of projects. Employers in the GCC increasingly value demonstrable skills over advanced degrees alone.
What programming language should I start with?
Python is the undisputed first choice for AI and data science. It has the richest ecosystem of libraries and is used by the vast majority of AI practitioners globally. Once you are comfortable with Python, SQL is the next most important language to learn for working with data.
How long does it take to become job-ready in AI?
With consistent effort of 10–15 hours per week, most students can reach an entry-level job-ready standard within 9–12 months alongside their degree. The key is building real projects and earning at least one recognised certification to complement your university qualification.
Are AI and ICT jobs available for graduates in the GCC?
Yes — the GCC tech sector actively recruits international and local talent, particularly for specialised AI roles where graduate pipelines are still developing. UAE, Qatar, and Saudi Arabia all have visa pathways designed for skilled technology workers, and Bahrain's Tamkeen programme offers direct support for Bahraini graduates.
Can I get funding for AI certifications in Bahrain?
Yes — Tamkeen in Bahrain offers significant subsidies for training and certification programmes for Bahraini nationals, including certifications from IBM, Microsoft, AWS, and Google. Support can cover up to 100% of training costs.
What is the difference between a Cyber Analyst and a Cybersecurity Specialist?
A Cyber Analyst is typically an entry-to-mid level role focused on monitoring, detecting, and responding to threats — ideal for students entering the field. A Cybersecurity Specialist is a more senior position involving the design and architecture of security systems, penetration testing, and leading security strategy. The Analyst role is the natural stepping stone to becoming a Specialist.
